What Does Our Patient Journey Framework (PJF) Do?

From Contextual Intelligence to Strategy Vectors: The Starting Point, Not the Destination

The PJF initial report represents the essential first step in strategy creation, not its conclusion. The framework's primary deliverable is the identification of Strategy Vectors—points of strategic intervention where behavioural changes can fundamentally redirect outcomes toward desired future states.

Understanding Strategy Vectors

Strategy Vectors are the synthesis of comprehensive market intelligence contextualised through the patient journey. Each vector represents:

  • A Current State - What behaviours exist today and why they persist
  • A Problem or Opportunity - Where current behaviours create suboptimal outcomes or where gaps reveal untapped potential
  • A Leverage Point - Where intervention could trigger cascading behavioural changes across multiple stakeholders
  • Multiple Possible Futures - Various pathways that could lead to preferred outcomes

The 12-Step Framework

Our proprietary framework integrates health economics, market dynamics, competitive intelligence, stakeholder mapping, and behavioural science into a cohesive strategic tool. This comprehensive approach ensures no critical factor is overlooked, creating a complete picture of the healthcare landscape.

Strategy Building Begins Here

Once Strategy Vectors are identified, teams can engage in the genuine work of strategy creation:

Debate Possibilities - Each Strategy Vector opens multiple pathways forward. Teams must explore, challenge, and refine these possibilities. The framework provides the structured foundation for these strategic debates.

Iterate with Your Team - Strategy development is not linear. Armed with Strategy Vectors teams can rapidly iterate on strategic concepts (& re-engage with our PJF!)

Dive into New Areas of Investigation - Strategy Vectors reveal gaps in understanding or areas requiring deeper investigation. This focused investigation is fundamentally different from broad data collection; it's targeted inquiry in service of strategic possibility. Aiming at real insight.

Seek Fresh Insight - The framework creates fertile ground for insight generation by revealing unexpected connections, contradictions, and opportunities. These insights emerge from the synthesis of contextual intelligence, not from the data itself.

Build Pilots and Scenarios - With Strategy Vectors identified and possibilities debated, teams can develop rapid pilots to test hypotheses and create scenarios to model different future states. What would have to be true for this Strategy Vector to shift?

Move Quickly with Strategy Deployment - Because the framework creates alignment around problems and possibilities, organizations can move from strategy creation to deployment with unusual speed. Teams aren't selling ideas across silos; they're executing against shared Strategy Vectors that everyone understands and believes in.
